Why attend?

Learn how modern DNA language models work and how to use them in your research.

Understand architecture

Tokenization, Embeddings, Transformer & Non-Transformer Architectures, MLM & autoregressive training

Generate embeddings

Extract biologically meaningful representations from any DNA sequence

Fine-tune efficiently

LoRA & QLoRA on limited hardware – focus on Mistral-DNA & NT variants

Target Audience

This is a fast-paced, intermediate level workshop designed for researchers and students with the following background.

Prerequisites

  • Working knowledge of Python (scripting, Jupyter notebooks)
  • Basic understanding of machine learning concepts (training, evaluation, overfitting)
  • Familiarity with molecular biology fundamentals (DNA, transcription, gene regulation)
  • Comfort using the command line and conda/pip environments

Who should attend

  • Bioinformaticians exploring deep learning for genomics
  • Computational biologists looking to apply pre-trained DNA models
  • ML/AI researchers interested in biological sequence modeling
  • Graduate students and postdocs in genomics or data science
